## 2. What Went Well

1. **The role-dispatch model held up.** Story 4-1 created the parse, transform, and emit seam for projection render strategies. Stories 4-2 through 4-6 reused that seam instead of rewriting role handling.
2. **Semantic badges became a reusable UI primitive.** Story 4-2 added `FcStatusBadge`, `SlotAppearanceTable`, HFC1025/HFC1026 diagnostics, localized fallback copy, and contrast assertions. Later stories reused the slot model for chips and status surfaces.
3. **DataGrid state finally became bidirectional.** Story 4-3 supplied the filter/search/sort actions, reserved keys, reducer/effect path, components, and shortcut scope that make the earlier `GridViewSnapshot` work useful.
4. **Virtualization shipped with lifecycle discipline.** Story 4-4 did the hard work around page-load actions, pending completions, cancellation, disposal, scroll capture, bounded cache behavior, and column visibility without changing the persistence blob shape.
5. **Expand-in-row composed existing detail rendering instead of inventing another detail surface.** Story 4-5 reused the Story 4-1 `DetailRecord` body and kept expanded-row state ephemeral, avoiding persistence schema churn.
6. **Unsupported data became visible.** Story 4-6 fixed a core honesty issue: unsupported projection fields no longer disappear at transform time. They now flow to `FcFieldPlaceholder` with build-time and runtime signals.
7. **The lessons ledger improved.** Epic 4 added useful reusable lessons: async-bridge lifecycle, JS interop disposal, bounded caches, and silent-drop guardrails.

Dana (QA Engineer): "The best pattern was the regression evidence around shared generator paths. When a story touched the emitter, it usually added approval baselines or focused guardrails."

Charlie (Senior Dev): "And the architecture stayed mostly additive. `GridViewSnapshot` did not become a dumping ground; reserved-key packing and ephemeral state kept the schema stable."

## 4. Key Lessons

1. **A story should not be marked behavior-complete when the generator does not emit the new behavior.** Story 4-3 shipped valuable infrastructure, but its own record says the generated `{TypeName}View` integration remains a 4-3.1 follow-up. That is a completion semantics problem, not an individual fault.
2. **Async bridge contracts need the full lifecycle in the first draft.** Story 4-4 improved after the pending completion lifecycle was made explicit: registration, cancellation, success, failure, disposal, terminal dispatch, nested safety, and null-payload handling.
3. **Visible placeholder fixes need pipeline guardrails.** Story 4-6 did the right thing by bundling transform-stage inclusion, emit-stage placeholder rendering, and a regression lesson. That pattern belongs in central guidance.
4. **Ephemeral state should stay out of persistence by default.** Expand-in-row proved this well: the user gets task-local detail without bloating the storage blob or creating surprising restore behavior.
5. **Browser-quality debt is accumulating in a useful but concentrated way.** Story 10-2 now owns many real verification needs: phone layout, forced-colors, visual specimens, RTL, density, and browser-level performance checks.
6. **Resource and localization decisions need a recurring checklist.** French copy, NBSP conventions, ICU pluralization, aria wording, and fallback labels appeared repeatedly. They should not be rediscovered per component.

Amelia (Developer): "The main theme is not that Epic 4 overreached. It is that completion gates need to distinguish infrastructure done, emitted behavior done, and browser-verified behavior done."

Murat (Test Architect): "Exactly. The test story is strong at unit and generator levels. The next quality jump is making the browser and specimen lane routine."

## 7. Significant Discoveries

1. **Story 4-3 is marked done while a core emitted-behavior path remains deferred.**
   - Impact: FR12 should be considered partially implemented until the generated projection views actually emit the filter/search/sort surface and query enrichment.
   - Recommended action: Create or promote Story 4-3.1 as an explicit tracked item, or fold it into the nearest active generator story with clear acceptance criteria.

2. **Epic 4 created a projection-interaction platform, not isolated UI components.**
   - Impact: `FcStatusBadge`, reserved grid keys, `LoadedPageState`, `ExpandedRowState`, `FcProjectionEmptyPlaceholder`, `FcFieldPlaceholder`, and role-dispatched emitters are now shared framework architecture.
   - Recommended action: Update architecture and central constraints before Epic 6 and quality stories depend on these seams.

3. **Visual/accessibility verification is now a real program, not a side note.**
   - Impact: Many Epic 4 stories defer phone viewport, forced-colors, RTL, density, and visual specimen validation to Story 10-2.
   - Recommended action: Make Story 10-2 an explicit consolidation story for Epic 3 and Epic 4 visual verification debt.

4. **The project learned a strong silent-drop prevention pattern.**
   - Impact: Story 4-6's L15 should become mandatory for future generator pipeline changes.
   - Recommended action: Add L15 to reviewer checklists and central guidance.

**Epic update required:** Yes, but not as a rewrite of completed Epic 4. Update follow-up tracking for 4-3.1 or equivalent, Story 4-7 candidates, Epic 6 seam planning, Story 9-4/9-5 governance and localization, and Story 10-2 visual/accessibility gates.
